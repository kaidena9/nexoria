Automation that saves time and tightens operations.

We build practical systems that route leads, automate follow-up, reduce manual work, and make day-to-day operations easier to manage — without bolting on tools nobody uses.

If your team is wasting hours on manual updates, copy-paste, or follow-up that never happens, automation can usually return that time within weeks.

01 — Common problems we solve

Where manual work quietly costs the business.

Most service businesses do not have a tooling problem. They have a connection problem. The tools exist; they just do not talk to each other, so people fill the gap by hand.

  • 01

    Leads sit before anyone responds.

    Inquiries come in through the website, social, or referrals. Nobody is notified in time, and the lead goes cold.

  • 02

    Quoting and onboarding eat hours.

    Each new client involves the same forms, the same emails, and the same spreadsheets, every time. Nothing is reused.

  • 03

    Follow-up depends on memory.

    There is no real follow-up sequence. Some leads get five touches. Most get none. Whichever the rep remembers.

  • 04

    Tools do not talk to each other.

    The CRM, the website forms, the marketing tools, and the calendar all hold pieces of the same information. Nobody has the whole picture.

02 — What we build

Practical systems, not science projects.

We focus on the systems that have the highest return for the smallest amount of new tooling: lead handling, follow-up, internal coordination, and reporting.

  • CRM setup and lead routing

    One CRM as the source of truth. Inbound leads tagged, assigned, and routed by source, geography, or service.

  • Instant lead notifications

    Email or SMS to the right person within seconds of a form submission, so nobody waits hours for the alert.

  • Follow-up workflows

    Consistent, multi-touch follow-up for new leads, stalled deals, and post-engagement check-ins.

  • Quoting and onboarding

    Templates, document generation, e-signature, and intake forms tied together so each new client follows the same clean path.

  • Tool integration

    Connecting the website, CRM, calendar, email, and ad platforms so a lead in one place updates everywhere.

  • Internal notifications

    Slack, email, or SMS triggers when something matters — new high-value lead, stalled proposal, anniversary, refund request.

  • Reporting dashboards

    One internal view of inbound volume, pipeline, response time, conversion, and team load.

  • Operational documentation

    Short, readable SOPs for the workflows we build so the team can run them without us in the room.

03 — System examples

The kinds of systems we build most often.

Below are common workflows we put in place for service businesses. Real engagements typically combine three or four of these.

  • 01

    Inbound lead workflow

    Website form → CRM record → instant alert to the right rep → first-touch email scheduled → calendar handoff → reporting.

  • 02

    Quoting and onboarding

    Intake form → auto-generated proposal → e-signature → client folder created → kickoff scheduled → team notified.

  • 03

    Nurture and re-engagement

    Lifecycle email tied to lead status, last activity, and prior conversations — without spam-style blasts.

  • 04

    Internal reporting

    A single dashboard with inbound volume, response time, conversion rate, pipeline value, and which campaigns drove what.

04 — Why manual processes cost growth

Manual work hides the real cost.

Manual processes look free because no software invoice shows up. The cost is paid in slower follow-up, missed inquiries, inconsistent customer experience, and the time your team is not spending on actual work.

  • Leads that wait more than an hour convert noticeably less.
  • Manual quoting puts a ceiling on how many clients you can take on.
  • Inconsistent follow-up loses the leads paid acquisition just delivered.
  • Team energy goes into copy-paste instead of customer outcomes.
  • Reporting that requires spreadsheet work each week stops happening.
  • New hires take longer to ramp because the process is undocumented.
  • Knowledge leaves when people leave because it lived in their head.
  • Decisions get made on impressions because data is hard to assemble.

05 — Engagement flow

How automation projects run.

Automation projects are usually scoped in tight phases. We pick the highest-impact workflow first, ship it, and then expand from there.

  • Highest-impact workflow shipped first
  • Team training built into delivery
  • Documentation included
  • No tool lock-in we cannot justify
  1. 01

    Map current state

    We walk through how leads come in, how they move, who touches what, and where time is being spent on the wrong things.

  2. 02

    Design the workflow

    We pick the one or two workflows with the most upside and design them clearly before building anything.

  3. 03

    Build and connect

    We configure tools, write integrations, and connect data so the workflow runs end-to-end without manual steps.

  4. 04

    Roll out and document

    The team gets walkthroughs and short written SOPs. The workflow becomes part of how the business runs.
